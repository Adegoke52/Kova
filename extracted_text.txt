1. Executive summary 
Kova is a mobile-first client experience platform built for small and micro businesses across 
Nigeria and, eventually, the world. It enables any business owner — a hairdresser, a plumber, a 
caterer, a lawyer — to send every client a beautifully branded, professional invoice and job 
summary page in under 30 seconds, directly through WhatsApp. 
 
The platform is designed around three non-negotiable principles for the Nigerian market: zero 
new habit required, lightweight enough to run on 3G, and visible results from the very first use. 
Clients receive a premium experience. Business owners build a professional reputation 
effortlessly. And every invoice sent becomes a quiet advertisement that spreads Kova 
organically. 
 
 North star:  What if every small business owner could make every client touchpoint feel like they 
hired a top-tier agency — without hiring anyone? 
A tool that helps you get paid faster and look professional doing itf 
2. Problem statement 
2.1 The perception gap 
Small business owners in Nigeria are skilled, hardworking, and deeply trusted within their 
communities. But the tools they use to communicate with clients — WhatsApp voice notes, 
handwritten receipts, PDF screenshots, verbal confirmations — make them look informal, even 
when the quality of their work is exceptional. 
 
This is not a visibility problem. It is a perception problem. The gap between how good these 
businesses actually are and how professional they appear to clients costs them repeat 
business, referrals, and the ability to charge what they are worth. 
 
2.2 Why existing tools fail 
• Website builders (Wix, Squarespace) require too much setup, ongoing maintenance, 
and design skill 
• Invoicing apps (Wave, QuickBooks) are desktop-heavy, slow, and not WhatsApp-native 
• Social media profiles are passive — they do not create direct client touchpoints after a 
job 
• Generic PDF invoices carry no brand identity and feel impersonal 
• Most tools require a learning curve that Nigerian SMB owners simply do not have time 
for

3.4 Moat & Defensibility Strategy 
Kova’s long-term advantage is built on a combination of data, reputation, network effects, 
and behavioral integration. 
As businesses use Kova, they accumulate invoice history, client records, earnings data, 
and reviews, making Kova their primary system of record. This creates strong data lock-in, 
as leaving the platform results in loss of business continuity and social proof. 
Kova also establishes a transaction-backed reputation layer, where every review is tied to 
a real job. This builds a trusted profile that increases a business’s ability to win clients and 
charge premium rates. 
Each invoice sent introduces new clients to Kova, creating organic network effects where 
professional, structured transactions become expected. Over time, this drives adoption 
through client-side demand. 
Additionally, Kova integrates directly into existing workflows via WhatsApp, creating 
behavioral lock-in by aligning with how businesses already operate. 
As data compounds, Kova can evolve into a financial identity layer, enabling access to 
insights, credit, and growth opportunities — positioning the platform as critical infrastructure 
for small businesses

10. Bot: 'What service(s) did you provide?' — owner types or selects from recent items

13. Owner taps to forward the link to their client on WhatsApp 
14. Entire flow: under 30 seconds 
 
6.3 Client — receiving and paying 
15. Client receives a WhatsApp message from the business owner with a Kova link 
16. Client opens link — sees a clean, branded invoice page 
17. Client reviews the job summary and total amount 
18. Client taps the pay button and completes payment via their preferred method 
19. Client receives payment confirmation on the page 
20. Client sees the one-tap review option — submits a rating if satisfied 
21. Client notices 'powered by Kova' and may explore the platform

10.1 Extended Risk Handling Framework 
Kova operates in a trust-sensitive market where risks extend beyond technical security into 
payment trust, identity integrity, and platform misuse. 
To mitigate payment-related risks, Kova introduces a “Payment Claimed” state supported by 
optional payment reference capture and quick verification actions for business owners. 
Payments are only confirmed after owner validation, ensuring clarity without introducing friction. 
To prevent abuse, all accounts are tied to OTP-verified phone numbers, with activity 
monitoring, rate limits, and anomaly detection applied to identify suspicious behavior. Over time, 
Kova will develop a trust scoring system to strengthen platform integrity. 
To reduce dependency on external platforms, Kova maintains a web-based fallback flow for 
invoice creation and access, ensuring continuity beyond WhatsApp constraints. 
Finally, all reviews are invoice-linked and transaction-bound, preventing manipulation and 
preserving the credibility of the platform’s reputation system.

2.2 Regulatory Tailwind — The FIRS e-Invoicing Mandate 
This is the most significant market development that is not addressed in the current PRD and 
represents both a major opportunity and a near-term risk. 
 
 CRITICAL CONTEXT 
Nigeria's FIRS has mandated e-invoicing for all VAT-registered businesses. Large taxpayers 
went live November 2025. Medium and small businesses entered mandatory compliance from 
January 2026. 68% of small businesses in Lagos were unaware of this mandate as of late 
2025. 
 
What this means for Kova: 
• Urgency to digitise is now government-mandated, not just aspirational. Kova enters a market 
where the 'why bother?' objection is increasingly answered by compliance pressure. 
• Nigeria's system uses Peppol BIS Billing 3.0 — a structured international standard. Kova's current 
invoice format is not compliant. This is a v1.1 priority. 
• The mandate creates a category education moment. Businesses actively searching for invoicing 
tools have surged. Kova's SEO and content strategy should pivot to capture this demand. 
• Enforcement timelines run through 2027-2028 for smaller businesses, giving Kova a window to 
integrate compliance before it becomes blocking.

SECTION 03 
Competitor Analysis 
Who is in the arena and how Kova wins 
 
3.1 Competitive Landscape Overview 
Kova operates at the intersection of three markets: invoicing tools, client experience platforms, and 
WhatsApp business tools. No single competitor occupies all three. This is Kova's strategic whitespace. 
 
 Strategic Whitespace 
There is no tool in the Nigerian market — or globally — that combines WhatsApp-native 
invoice creation, branded client experience pages, real-time open notifications, and a 
reputation layer in a single 30-second flow. That is Kova's moat.

SECTION 04 
Stakeholder Interview Guide 
The questions that will sharpen Kova before launch 
 
4.1 How to Use This Guide 
These interview guides are designed for three distinct stakeholder groups: Potential Business Owners 
(primary users), Clients of small businesses (secondary users / virality test), and Investors / Advisors. 
Each guide is structured to uncover jobs-to-be-done, pain depth, and willingness to pay — not feature 
preferences. 
 
 Interview Principle 
Never ask: 'Would you use this feature?' Always ask: 'Tell me about the last time you sent an 
invoice. Walk me through exactly what happened.' Behaviour over opinion. Story over 
preference. 
 
4.2 Business Owner Interview Guide 
Profile to recruit 
Lagos-based service business owner, 1-5 employees, actively uses WhatsApp for business. Mix of 
beauty, food, home services, and freelancers. 60% female. Ages 24-42. Sessions: 45 minutes. Format: 
In-person or WhatsApp voice call. 
 
Opening (5 min) — Build rapport, establish context 
1. Tell me about your business — what do you do, how long have you been doing it, and how many 
clients do you serve in a typical month? 
2. Walk me through a typical week. When do you have the most clients? What does your busiest 
day look like? 
 
Current Invoicing Behaviour (10 min) — Map the current state 
3. When you finish a job, how do you collect payment? Walk me through exactly what happens from 
the moment the job is done. 
4. Do you send any kind of receipt or summary to clients? Show me what it looks like if you can. 
5. Have you ever had a client not pay, or delay payment? What happened? How did you follow up? 
6. How many invoices or receipts do you send in a week? How long does each one take? 
7. Have you ever lost a client or a referral because someone else looked more professional than 
you? What happened?

5.2 Design Imperatives — From an Awwwards-Level Lens 
The product vision is premium. The execution must match it at every pixel. Here are the non-
negotiables: 
 
• The client page IS the product. It is the one thing every single person touches. It must be 
extraordinary. Not good. Not clean. Extraordinary. Every visual decision on that page either 
validates the business owner's investment in Kova or undermines it. 
• Motion and micro-interactions matter enormously on a reputation product. When a client opens 
the page, it should feel like receiving something premium in the post. The load animation, the 
invoice reveal, the pay button — each should have intentional motion. 
• The 'paid' state deserves a celebration moment. When payment is confirmed, the page should 
transform. The business owner's WhatsApp notification should feel joyful. These moments build 
the dopamine loop that drives retention. 
• Brand colour application on client pages must feel premium, not garish. The 12 preset colour 
options should all look beautiful with the Kova layout — if even one looks bad, users will distrust 
the tool. 
• Typography hierarchy on the client page is load-bearing. On a 3G Android device, a well-set 
typographic hierarchy will render before images load. The page must be readable and compelling 
in its skeletal state.

5.3 The One Metric That Matters Most 
 North Star Metric 
Percentage of invoiced jobs that result in payment within 48 hours. This single number 
captures product-market fit (did the client page work?), payment friction (is the pay flow 
easy?), and business owner value (are they getting paid faster?). Everything else is 
secondary. 
 
5.4 Final Assessment 
Kova has the ingredients of a genuinely important product. The problem is real, the distribution insight 
is elegant, the retention architecture is thoughtful, and the market timing — with the FIRS mandate 
creating urgency — is exceptional. 
 
The risk is execution quality. In a market where trust is earned through perception, a single ugly client 
page, a slow-loading invoice, or a broken payment link will not just lose a user — it will embarrass the 
business owner in front of their client. The bar for quality is existential, not aspirational. 
 
If the team can maintain 30-second invoice creation, sub-2-second page loads on 3G, and a client-
page design that genuinely makes people say 'wow' — Kova will grow through word of mouth alone. 
 
 Final Word 
The best products in emerging markets don't just solve a problem. They give people dignity. 
Kova has a genuine shot at doing that for millions of small business owners across Nigeria 
and beyond. Build it like that matters. Because it does.
